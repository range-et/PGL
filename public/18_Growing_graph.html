Example 18 – Growing torus (live Kamada–Kawai)

A torus mesh (144 vertices, quad faces) grows one vertex at a time while Kamada–Kawai runs continuously. The torus's ring-of-rings connectivity produces a distinctive cylindrical structure that emerges as nodes are added. Existing node positions are preserved by seeding the new simulation's Float32Array directly.
